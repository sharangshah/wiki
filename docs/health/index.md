# Health

Ancient wisdom cross-referenced with modern evidence — Ayurveda, nutrition, and fitness.

This domain treats health as a system, not a collection of isolated interventions. The goal is to find principles that are robust across both traditional knowledge systems (particularly Ayurveda) and contemporary clinical evidence.

---

## Sections

**[Ayurveda](ayurveda/index.md)** — Constitutional types, seasonal routines, herbal protocols, and the Ayurvedic lens on chronic health.

**[Nutrition](nutrition/index.md)** — Macro and micronutrient frameworks, dietary patterns, food-as-medicine, and the evidence base for common claims.

**[Fitness](fitness/index.md)** — Training principles, recovery, longevity-focused movement, and the interplay between exercise and metabolic health.

---

## Epistemic Standard

Health is a domain riddled with confident claims and weak evidence. Every page here applies the epistemic filter: claims are graded by evidence quality, traditional knowledge is presented as traditional knowledge (not repackaged as clinical fact), and "we don't know yet" is stated explicitly where applicable.
